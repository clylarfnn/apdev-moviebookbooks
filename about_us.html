About MovieBookBooks

MovieBookBooks was founded in 1999 in Pangasinan as a movie rental business by three friends. MovieBookBooks founders Clyla Rafanan, Dani Solis, and Kyla Lacson came up with the idea of as movie rental service that allowed users to order movies from a website and have the DVDs be delivered via mail after hearing the complaints of friends who did not live near a video rental store. Since then, it has evolved into a move distribution company for Philippine cinemas. Since its transition from a movie rental business in 2007, MovieBookBooks has been the main distributor for local and foreign films for numerous cinemas in the country.

Meet The Founders

Kyla Lacson - Chief Executive Officer

Kyla Lacson graduated with a degree in B.S Information Systems from De La Salle University-Manila. She worked as a junior software developer at CARPS before being invited to start MovieBookBooks. She is proud to be from Western Visayas, the home of the Masskara Festival of the Philippines. Aside from studying, coding, and making milkshakes, she likes cats and zombies. She dislikes zombie cats.

Dani Solis - Chief Operating Officer

Dani Solis graduated with a degree in B.S Information Technology with a Minor in Marketing Strategy from De La Salle University-Manila. She initially worked as an Applications Developer at RIBS before starting MovieBookBooks with her friends. Her interest in films started at the age of 5 when she and her grandparents would have a weekly traditon of watching movies every Friday night back in Davao. In fact, it was her grandparents' complaints of having no video rentals store that caused Dani to pitch MovieBookBooks to her friends. When she's not working on business-related things, she can be seen collecting KPOP merchandise and walking her pet dog, Coco.

Clyla Rafanan - Chief Administrative Officer

Clyla Rafanan graduated with a degree in B.S Business Management with a Minor in Social Technopreneurship from De La Salle University-Manila. Before working on MovieBookBooks with Dani and Kyla, she worked as a front-end developer at STARBS. She often wonders why she wasn’t born with incredible dancing skills. If you want to get in touch with her, find something glittery and you’ll surely catch her eye.
